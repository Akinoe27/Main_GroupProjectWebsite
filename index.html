Organic Backyard Farming for Everyone: The Future of Food

Organic farming, is a first step to combat social issues that we are facing like hunger and if this becomes a household practice, it can maximize the production and minimize the cost of food with little regard to impacts on the environment. As the world enters an era in which global food production is likely to double, it is critical that agricultural practices be modified to minimize environmental impacts even though many such practices are likely to increase the costs of production.

More on Organic Farming




Vermiculture

Means artificial rearing or cultivation of worms (Earthworms) and the technology is the scientific process of using them for the betterment of human beings. Vermicompost is the excreta of earthworm, which is rich in humus. It has a lot of beneficial components like maintaining Ph balance as well as retaining soil’s moisture content which is ideal for tropical countries like in the Philippines. Unlike the normal Urea fertilizer, it doesn’t destroy the soil’s living organism but rather, helping them to thrive in the world’s changing temperature. Ultimately, this method helps our growing needs while giving respect to soil’s original structure. You can check the article below for complete instructions https://growingspaces.com/what-is-vermiculture/



Aquaponics

It is a cooperation between plants and fish and the term originates from the two words aquaculture (the growing of fish in a closed environment) and hydroponics (the growing of plants usually in a soil-less environment).This method is hitting 2 birds in 1 stone; maintaining your home-grown fish, clean and healthy while growing your own food without excess usage of water and additional cost for filters. For more tips on how to start, follow the link below: https://gogreenaquaponics.com/blogs/news/what-is-aquaponics-and-how-does-it-work


Organic pigs

It is going back to the basic way of raising pigs in a more liberating and non-restrictive environment where they can do whatever they want. It has access to outdoors, shade, shelter, exercise areas, fresh air, clean drinking water, and direct sunlight as suitable for the animal's stage of life, the climate, and the environment.Historically, pigs are just roaming around the earth and have proven to have a vital role in micro-organisms due to their way of living. Organic chicken

It has access to an abundance of fresh air, daylight and outdoor space. These chickens are fed only organic feed that is adapted to this slow-growing breed. Similar to Organic pigs, they are also known to be found in the wild and plays a vital role in the food chain. The most practical way of raising chickens is letting them live in a non-restrictive environment with the ability to feed on their own. This way, it is proven that their egg-produce is found to be healthier and it less expensive. For more information, you can follow the link below:

https://www.hgtv.com/outdoors/gardens/animals-and-wildlife/how-to-raise-an-organic-chicken

Hydroponics

Is the technique of growing plants using a water-based nutrient solution rather than soil, and can include an aggregate substrate, or growing media, such as vermiculite , coconut coir, or perlite. Hydroponic production systems are used by small farmers, hobbyists, and commercial enterprises. Unlike the Aquaponics, it nurture plants, under water treatment but with no fish beneath involved.This method produce a greater yield of fruits and vegetables because in a hydroponic system plants are more densely spaced together compared to the size of land that would be needed to grow the same number of plants. For more information, you can follow the link below: https://www.freshwatersystems.com/blogs/blog/what-are-hydroponic-systems

Organic farming, also known as ecological farming or biological farming, is an agricultural system that uses fertilizers of organic origin such as compost manure, green manure, and bone meal and places emphasis on techniques such as crop rotation and companion planting. It originated early in the 20th century in reaction to rapidly changing farming practices. Certified organic agriculture accounts for 70 million hectares (170 million acres) globally, with over half of that total in Australia. Organic farming continues to be developed by various organizations today. Biological pest control, mixed cropping, and the fostering of insect predators are encouraged. Organic standards are designed to allow the use of naturally-occurring substances while prohibiting or strictly limiting synthetic substances. For instance, naturally-occurring pesticides such as pyrethrin are permitted, while synthetic fertilizers and pesticides are generally prohibited. Synthetic substances that are allowed include, for example, copper sulfate, elemental sulfur, and ivermectin. Genetically modified organisms, nanomaterials, human sewage sludge, plant growth regulators, hormones, and antibiotic use in livestock husbandry are prohibited. Organic farming advocates claim advantages in sustainability, openness, self-sufficiency, autonomy and independence,[11] health, food security, and food safety.
